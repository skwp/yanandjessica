About Jessica

Jessica grew up on the mean streets of Wilmette, raised by Rafail and Lena Ogulnik, where she developed a penchant for gourmet cooking and a fondness for gangster rap, something she would later find strangely in common with Yan. Her genuine love for kids drove her into a director of activities position at Niles West High School, where hundreds of kids greet her in the hallways like one of their own. Jessica packs her days to the brim with adventure. She's an avid traveler and has been all over America and many foreign lands, including a stint as a Fulbright Scholar in Argentina, where she fell in love with Yoga. She loves to laugh and cook, and puts on a mean party. Her bubbly personality and gourmet culinary skills draw in huge numbers of friends at her annual Latkefest. Jessica secretly harbors many artistic talents including playing the flute, drawing naked people, pottery, and scrapbooking.

About Yan

Yan is an extreme adventurer at heart, a quality that was instilled by his parents, Yury and Svetlana. He was born in Kiev, Ukraine, and arrived to Skokie, IL early in his childhood. He grew up skiing powder backwards, climbing walls and crevasses, camping in extreme environments, and eventually forayed into photography and software startups. Yan also has a healthy fascination with linguistics, as well as the underground cultural arts and foodie scene. He is hopelessly in love with Burning Man and has infected most of his friends with this love. He plays guitar, sings, and is exploring the piano and harmonica. Yan is also an organizer extraordinaire, and plans vacations for himself and his friends on a regular basis. He loves to read books about behavioral economics, as well as watch entire science fiction television series in one sitting.
